From Shieldbearer

An Open Letter

To Christian musicians who believe talent makes them important. This is a necessary conversation.

There is a growing attitude in the Christian music world. An unspoken belief that musical talent makes you spiritually superior. That skill earns you the right to look down on other believers. That years of training place you above those who create differently. This is pride. And pride is always the beginning of a fall.

The Parable You Keep Ignoring

Matthew 20:1-16. Jesus tells a story about a man hiring workers throughout the day. Some started early. Some arrived later. Some barely worked an hour. At the end of the day, the master pays them all the same. The early workers were furious. The master replies: you already received what you agreed to. I have the right to be generous. Your anger is rooted in envy.

People get angry not because something wrong happened, but because someone else received a blessing they did not think they deserved. This is exactly how some Christian musicians respond when God uses people with less training, fewer skills, or different tools.

Why Some of You Fear Technology

Some musicians are not afraid of AI. They are afraid of competition. They are afraid of losing their monopoly. They are afraid that the field is now level and anyone can create. Not because they are protecting the gospel. But because they are protecting their ego.

God Has Never Been Impressed By Talent

  • Moses said he could not speak well. God used him anyway.
  • David was not the obvious choice. Even his family ignored him. God did not.
  • Gideon said he was the weakest in the weakest family. God turned him into a leader.
  • Amos was a farmer. God still called him.
  • Peter and the Apostles were uneducated, ordinary men. God gave them boldness and authority.
  • Paul said he was the least worthy. God used him to write most of the New Testament.
1 Corinthians 3:7

"Neither the one who plants nor the one who waters is anything, but only God who gives the growth."

2 Corinthians 12:9

"My power is made perfect in weakness."

If you despise believers whom God chooses to use. If you mock those with less skill. If you attack those who create differently. If you try to muzzle those God has empowered. You are not safeguarding righteousness. You are resisting the work of God.


Repent. Return to humility. Talent is a gift. Not a throne. Not a crown. Not a right to judge others. God can raise up anyone. With training or without it. With talent or without it. With traditional tools or with modern ones.

In Christ — Shieldbearer
