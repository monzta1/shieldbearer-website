Lyrics and Meaning

Every lyric is intentional. Every theme is rooted in Scripture. Open each track to read the words, meaning, and references in full.

Christ is not hidden in metaphor here. Scripture is the source, not the decoration. Each song begins with a text and works outward into sound, emotion, and weight. This archive is designed to scale as the catalog grows, and now it can be browsed by genre as well as theme.

Broken Helicopter

Track 09 · Country · Fatherhood Story Ballad

A small domestic moment turned into a testimony about fatherhood, time, and the love that keeps showing up.

Tags: Country, Fatherhood, Story, Family

Meaning

A little kid walks up with a broken toy helicopter and asks, “Dada, can you fix it?” That moment becomes the whole song.

Broken Helicopter is built around fatherhood, time, and the love that never stops trying to mend what is broken. From fixing a toy on the floor to cheering from the sidelines, the song follows the long arc of a father staying present.

It is a story every parent will feel somewhere in the chest: the child changes, the questions change, the battles change, but the instinct to help, hold, and steady them does not.

He Found His Voice

Track 10 · Country · Autism Testimony · Psalm 40:1-3

A testimony song for Leo and for every family waiting, praying, and refusing to give up.

Tags: Country, Testimony, Family, Hope

Meaning

Our son Leo was diagnosed with autism before school. For years, we were not sure if he would ever speak clearly. He lined up toys instead of using words, and there were long stretches of silence while other kids seemed to run ahead.

But God was faithful. Through prayer, patience, and the tireless love of his teachers, Leo found his voice. Today he is thriving in math, raising his hand in class, and shining with the gifts God has placed in him.

This song is dedicated to his journey and to every teacher who saw the spark behind his eyes and refused to give up. The musician’s note matters too: this track was built around clean guitar on an all-rosewood Telecaster, shaped with parallel compression, chorus, delay, and reverb so the vocal stays front and center.

Scripture Source

Psalm 40:1-3 — "I waited patiently for the Lord; he turned to me and heard my cry. He lifted me out of the slimy pit, out of the mud and mire; he set my feet on a rock and gave me a firm place to stand. He put a new song in my mouth, a hymn of praise to our God."

Lyrics

So we held on, we prayed and hoped, and followed every little trail.

And now you're counting stars in class, solving puzzles, moving fast.

And now he's counting stars in class, solving puzzles, moving fast.

Speech was once a distant dream, now he sings and builds and beams.
